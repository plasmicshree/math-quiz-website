## Key Observations

### Progression Pattern
1. **Grade 1-2:** Visual blocks for concrete learning (5 and 10 sections)
2. **Grade 3:** Introduce larger numbers and multiplication tables
3. **Grade 4:** Add division (whole numbers)
4. **Grade 5:** Division with remainders, fractions with same denominator
5. **Grade 6:** Complex division, fractions with different denominators

### Safety Measures
- ✅ **No Negatives:** All subtraction ensures minuend ≥ subtrahend
- ✅ **Whole Division (Grade 4):** Ensures perfect division (remainder = 0)
- ✅ **Grade 5-6 Division:** Allows remainders for realistic division practice
- ✅ **Fractions:** Grade 5 (same denominator) before Grade 6 (different denominators)

### Visualization Strategy
- **Grades 1-2:** Block-based visualization (helps concrete understanding)
- **Grades 3-4:** Text-based (transition to abstract thinking)
- **Grade 5-6:** Pie chart visualization for fractions (visual representation of parts)

## Implementation Notes

- All generators use `random.randint()` for variety
- Division ensures whole results where specified
- Fractions convert to decimal with 2-place rounding
- Word problem templates loaded from CSV for Grades 4+
- Visual blocks use dynamic SVG rendering based on section count
- Block utilities (`create_blocks()`) handle visual generation
